Experience

Programming

The main programming language I have experience in is Python (both Linux and Windows environment). However, I have had exposure to other languages during my education and work (MATLAB, some C++). In my current job, the programming I do is predominantly for data analysis and numerical modelling, using libraries such as numpy and pandas.

Outside of my full-time job, I have been developing my knowledge of other areas of programming and working on my own projects, covering a broad cross-section of languages and tools, such as HTML, CSS, web development using Flask, SQLite, PostgreSQL, REST APIs (interaction and design), web scraping and automation (Beautiful Soup/ Selenium), GUIs, SMTP, data visualisation with seaborn.

I am happy to work in my own free time to learn new programming languages and frameworks as needed and consider programming a hobby, which is why I would like to pivot my career into software development for the long-term.

Geophysics

My current full-time job (geophysicist) is a client facing role in which I help large energy companies from Europe, Africa and the Middle East to discover new hydrocarbon reserves and find reservoirs deep inside the earth for trapping CO2 from the atmosphere.

This is done using a powerful Linux workstation, a 250 petaflop HPC cluster and a range of skills (e.g. physics, maths, signal processing, programming, quantitative analysis, numerical modelling) to image the inside of the earth and detect anomalies.

Projects are typically several months long and must be completed to strict deadlines due to the high costs involved. Regular contact with clients is maintained through a combination of weekly technical presentations, review meetings, written reports and emails.
